About Me



I am Mohit, a Ph.D. candidate in the Sustainable School of Engineering & the Built Environment (SSEBE) at Arizona State University. My PhD research uses computer vision to digitize images of engineering blueprints and leverages Natural Language Processing to design a Question-Answering mechanism to automate manual process of design review and compliance process.
During Ph.D., I got opportunities to work with researchers from Stanford University (US), TU Munich (Germany) and EPFL (Switzerland) on research projects related to video analysis with CNNs & LSTMs, 3D reconstruction with deep-learning based methods like Neural Radiance Fields.
I also worked with indutry partners such as Salt River Project for IoT based condition monitoring of assets, Sundt Construction for Document Digitization, Penta Building Group for building a LLM-based chatbot based on historical data of construction projects.
Both the research and industry projects provided me with a unique opportunity to work on real-world problems and develop solutions that can be applied in practice.
This motivated me to even launch a start-up called Street Counts, a PaaS (Platform as a Service) company providing computer vision-based Traffic Management solutions to cities and municipalities (and raised about ~ $135k in seed grants).

My advisors at ASU are - Dr. Thomas Czerniawski, Dr. Ricardo Eiris, and Dr. Deborah Salon.
